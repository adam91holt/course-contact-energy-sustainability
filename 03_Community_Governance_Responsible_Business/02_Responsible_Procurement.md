# Responsible Procurement and Supply Chain

Contact Energy's sustainability commitment extends beyond our own operations to our entire supply chain. Through responsible procurement, we ensure our suppliers share our values and help us achieve our ESG goals. This lesson explores how we manage sustainability in our supply chain.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain Contact Energy's approach to responsible procurement
- Describe the ESG Programme for our supply chain
- Understand how we identify and manage supply chain risks
- Recognise the role of the Supplier Code of Conduct

### The Scale of Our Supply Chain

Contact works with hundreds of suppliers providing:
- Equipment and materials for power stations
- Construction and maintenance services
- Professional and consulting services
- Office supplies and technology
- Fuel and energy inputs

### Why It Matters

- **Scope 3 emissions**: Our supply chain is a significant source of indirect emissions
- **Ethical standards**: We want suppliers who share our values
- **Risk management**: Supply chain issues can affect our operations and reputation
- **Stakeholder expectations**: Customers and investors expect responsible sourcing

> **Key Principle:** ESG outcomes are built into our DNA at Contact, and we expect the same commitment from our suppliers.

## Our Responsible Procurement Framework

Contact has established a comprehensive approach to responsible procurement:

### Responsible Procurement Principles

Our principles set expectations for suppliers across key areas:

| Principle | What It Covers |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Environmental** | Emissions, waste, water, biodiversity |
| **Labour practices** | Fair wages, working conditions, safety |
| **Human rights** | Modern slavery prevention, discrimination |
| **Business ethics** | Anti-corruption, transparency, compliance |
| **Community impact** | Local engagement, social responsibility |

### Supplier Code of Conduct

All suppliers are expected to comply with our Supplier Code of Conduct, which sets out minimum standards for:

- **Environmental responsibility**: Minimising environmental impact
- **Workplace safety**: Providing safe working conditions
- **Fair labour**: Respecting worker rights and fair treatment
- **Ethical business**: Operating with integrity
- **Legal compliance**: Meeting all applicable laws and regulations

## ESG Programme for Supply Chain

Contact has established a dedicated ESG Programme for our supply chain to ensure suppliers' values and practices align with ours.

### Programme Objectives

1. Ensure supplier alignment with our Responsible Procurement Principles
2. Support our Contact26 strategy and sustainability goals
3. Identify and manage ESG risks including modern slavery
4. Drive continuous improvement in supply chain sustainability

### Governance

The Supply Chain ESG Programme is:
- **Managed by**: Senior Procurement and Contracting team
- **Overseen by**: Leadership Team
- **Aligned with**: Contact26 strategy and ESG commitments

### Risk Identification

We identify potential sustainability risks through:

**Significant Supplier Analysis**
- Identify top 100 suppliers based on spend
- Assess for potential ESG risks
- Prioritise for detailed review

**New Supplier Screening**
- All newly created suppliers are screened
- Responsible Procurement (ESG) Questionnaire
- Declaration form confirming commitment to standards

## Responsible Procurement Questionnaire

When onboarding new suppliers or reviewing existing ones, we use our Responsible Procurement (ESG) Questionnaire to assess:

### Environmental Factors
- Environmental management systems
- Emissions measurement and reduction
- Waste management practices
- Water and resource efficiency
- Biodiversity considerations

### Social Factors
- Health and safety management
- Worker rights and fair treatment
- Diversity and inclusion practices
- Community engagement
- Training and development

### Governance Factors
- Business ethics and anti-corruption
- Legal and regulatory compliance
- Risk management systems
- Transparency and reporting
- Grievance mechanisms

## Modern Slavery Risk Management

Modern slavery is a serious global issue, and Contact is committed to preventing it in our supply chain.

### What is Modern Slavery?

Modern slavery includes:
- Forced labour
- Human trafficking
- Debt bondage
- Child labour
- Deceptive recruiting practices

### Our Approach

| Action | How We Address It |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Risk assessment** | Evaluate suppliers for modern slavery risk |
| **Due diligence** | Investigate high-risk suppliers |
| **Contractual requirements** | Include anti-slavery clauses |
| **Monitoring** | Ongoing review of supplier practices |
| **Reporting** | Transparent reporting on our efforts |

### High-Risk Areas

We pay particular attention to supply chains that may have elevated risk:
- Manufacturing in certain regions
- Labour-intensive industries
- Construction and maintenance services
- Complex multi-tier supply chains

## Supplier Assessment and Monitoring

### Initial Assessment

When engaging new suppliers:
1. Complete Responsible Procurement Questionnaire
2. Sign declaration of commitment
3. Review for significant ESG risks
4. Include appropriate contract clauses
5. Plan for ongoing monitoring

### Ongoing Monitoring

For existing suppliers:
- Annual review of significant suppliers
- Updates to risk assessments
- Performance feedback and improvement
- Issue resolution processes
- Periodic re-certification

### Continuous Improvement

We work with suppliers to improve sustainability:
- Share best practices
- Provide feedback on performance
- Support capability development
- Recognise strong performers

## Supply Chain Emissions

Our supply chain contributes to Scope 3 emissions—the largest and most challenging category to address.

### Key Emission Sources

| Source | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Purchased goods and services** | Manufacturing emissions |
| **Capital goods** | Equipment production |
| **Fuel and energy activities** | Upstream emissions |
| **Transportation** | Freight and logistics |
| **Business travel** | Flights and accommodation |

### How We're Addressing This

- Preference for low-carbon suppliers
- Consideration of emissions in procurement decisions
- Engagement with suppliers on emissions reduction
- Tracking and reporting supply chain emissions

## Ethical Business Practices

We expect all suppliers to operate ethically:

### Anti-Corruption

- Zero tolerance for bribery and corruption
- Clear policies on gifts and hospitality
- Transparent business dealings
- Compliance with anti-corruption laws

### Fair Competition

- Honest tendering and bidding
- No price-fixing or market manipulation
- Transparent pricing and terms
- Respect for competitors

### Conflicts of Interest

- Disclosure of potential conflicts
- Management of related-party relationships
- Independent decision-making
- Transparency in supplier selection

## Supplier Diversity

Contact values supplier diversity and seeks to:
- Support Māori-owned businesses
- Engage local suppliers where possible
- Consider social enterprises
- Promote diversity in our supply base

### Benefits of Supplier Diversity

- Supports local economic development
- Brings diverse perspectives and innovation
- Strengthens community relationships
- Aligns with our social responsibility goals

### Procurement Team

- Conduct supplier assessments
- Monitor supplier performance
- Address issues and drive improvement

### Budget Holders

### ESG Reports
