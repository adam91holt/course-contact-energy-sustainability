# Community Partnerships and Engagement

Contact Energy operates across approximately 45 sites throughout New Zealand. Our presence in communities brings both responsibility and opportunity—responsibility to minimise our impact and opportunity to contribute positively to the places where we work. This lesson explores our approach to community engagement and the partnerships that make a difference.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:
- Explain Contact Energy's approach to community engagement
- Describe key community partnerships and conservation initiatives
- Understand the importance of neighbour relations
- Identify ways Contact supports local communities

## Our Community Commitment

Contact recognises that our actions impact the people and environment around us. Our neighbours—residents and businesses near our operations—are among our most important stakeholders.

![Community engagement](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1559027615-cd4628902d4a?w=800)

### Our Priorities

1. **Supporting communities**: Investing back into the communities where we operate
2. **Avoiding adverse impacts**: Minimising negative effects from our operations
3. **Mitigating issues**: Addressing concerns quickly and effectively
4. **Building relationships**: Developing genuine, long-term partnerships

> **Key Commitment:** All Contact Energy sites have community engagement plans in place, ensuring consistent, proactive engagement with our neighbours.

## Community Engagement Plans

Every Contact site maintains a community engagement plan that covers:

### Neighbour Communication
- Regular updates about operations

### Issue Resolution
- Clear processes for addressing complaints
- Timely responses to concerns

- Continuous improvement based on feedback

### Community Investment
- Identification of local needs
- Sponsorship and support opportunities
- Volunteer and participation activities
- Long-term partnership development

## Conservation Partnerships

Contact partners with conservation organisations to protect New Zealand's unique wildlife and ecosystems.

### Kiwi Contact Partnership

Contact partners with Save the Kiwi and Wairakei Golf + Sanctuary to protect New Zealand's iconic kiwi:

| Partner | Focus | Activity |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Save the Kiwi** | National kiwi protection | Partnership and support |
| **Wairakei Golf + Sanctuary** | Local kiwi habitat | Site conservation |
| **Taranaki Kiwi Trust** | Taranaki region | Education and advocacy |

### Taranaki Kiwi Trust Partnership

Since 2020, Contact has worked with the Taranaki Kiwi Trust:
- Sponsored Koko, a resident kiwi on Taranaki Maunga
- In 2025, extended to a three-year sponsorship
- Supports education and advocacy programmes
- Helps raise awareness of kiwi conservation

> **Why It Matters:** Kiwi are a national icon, but their populations remain vulnerable. Our support helps ensure these unique birds survive for future generations.

### Karearea Release Programme

Contact supports the release of New Zealand's native falcon, the karearea, on Tūwharetoa iwi land in Taupō:

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Species** | Karearea (NZ Falcon) |
| **Location** | Tūwharetoa iwi land, Taupō |
| **Released so far** | 6 birds |
| **Target** | 15 birds total |
| **Wild population** | Fewer than 10,000 remain |

This partnership exemplifies our approach:
- Working with iwi partners
- Supporting threatened species
- Combining conservation with cultural connection
- Creating lasting environmental benefits

## Environmental Community Projects

### Greening Taupō

Greening Taupō is a conservation group that has planted over 200,000 native trees with community support. Contact's partnership began in 2013:

**Our Contribution:**
- Provided 3,000 seedlings to kick off the project
- Sponsored annual community planting days
- Ongoing support for native restoration

**Community Impact:**
- Thousands of volunteers engaged
- Improved environment for people and wildlife
- Enhanced community pride and connection
- Long-term habitat restoration

### WAI Wānaka Partnership

Contact has partnered with WAI Wānaka to strengthen freshwater and biodiversity projects:

| Focus Area | Activities |
|------------|------------|
| **Water monitoring** | Tracking lake and river health |
| **Ecological restoration** | Riparian and wetland restoration |
| **Advocacy** | Supporting alpine lake protection |
| **Community engagement** | Education and volunteer programmes |

This partnership supports the health of Wānaka's precious alpine lakes and surrounding ecosystems.

## How We Engage

### Proactive Communication

We don't wait for issues to arise—we communicate proactively:
- Regular newsletters and updates
- Community meetings and information sessions
- Open days at our facilities
- Social media and digital communication

### Responsive Engagement

When community members have concerns, we respond promptly:
- Dedicated contact points at each site
- Clear escalation processes
- Transparent communication about issues
- Follow-up to ensure satisfaction

### Collaborative Approach

We work alongside communities, not just for them:
- Seeking input on projects and plans
- Incorporating community feedback
- Supporting community-led initiatives
- Building genuine partnerships
